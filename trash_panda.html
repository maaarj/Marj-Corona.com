Trash Panda redesign

Summary

I did a partial redesign of the app to improve the navigation and discoverability of information. I also initiated designing the features currently not implemented in the app.

Project Type
  • Personal project
  • Unsolicited redesign
Roles
  • Ux researcher
  • UX designer
  • UI designer

Trash Panda

After looking through options on how I can properly dispose my recyclables, I came across Trash Panda on Facebook. They offer pick up service for recyclables which is timely and useful during Covid-19 lockdown.

I began my case study by doing a brand analysis on Trash panda by going through their website and Facebook page. Through this, I was able to learn about their customers and competitors.

Why the redesign?

Although the app is pretty straightforward and easy to use, the in-app experience starts to plunge when users are doing the research before considering Trash Panda. Users will not hesitate to abandon their tasks or uninstall the application if they can't find the information they needed right away. It can result in users leaving them for their competitors.

Furthermore, I discovered that there is no means for the customer to cancel their booking within the app. This may not affect the overall user experience but it can have a huge impact on business resources since customers don't need to pay fees upfront.

Objectives for the redesign

  1. Improve information discoverability.
  2. Test features to identify if it further improve the customer experience.

Personal Objectives

  1. Take full ownership of the various roles involved in designing an app.
  2. Enhance my learning experience by carrying out UX activities and challenging design decisions through testing.

Scope and Limitation

Since Trash panda is still new to the market, recruiting participants is limited to their Facebook page. I stuck, as much as possible, with recruiting their user base to understand their unique personality and identify pain points.

To avoid any unforeseen problem, I also made a prototype design of the current app to let users freely explore or access parts of the app without waiting for available schedules.

The users

Trash panda’s target audience is 22-35 year-olds that have high environmental awareness.

Persona

I put a face on Trash Panda’s target audience to easily understand their motivations, problems, and personality and brainstorm suitable solutions.

The Problem

After analyzing the data, these are the problems that surfaced among the users and Trash panda:

Problem #1 - Information Discoverability

Inquiries about accepted recyclables and collection fees are evident on their Facebook page. I also noticed that 2 of the usability participants were confused or is expecting to see a dedicated area to easily access the accepted recyclables and collection fees.

Problem #2 - Booking Cancellation

Without the ability to systematically cancel booking:

  • Users face minor inconvenience by reaching out and waiting for a response from support on Facebook page.
  • The inefficiency of the method for employees to manually scan through messages for any cancellations.

This could result in poor resource management which can greatly impact Trash Panda.

Problem #3 - System status & Feedback

One usability participant pointed out that whenever she books for a pick up she’s a bit worried whether she actually secured her slot. She stated this from her experience with other services.

Confirmations emails are sent only a day before the collection day and send text messages if the collectors are nearby.

Inquiries and concern are also evident on their Facebook page.

UX Suggestions

#1 - Information discoverability

Bottom nav & Hamburger menu - to let the users feel in control in navigation. Most important information like Collection fee and List of recyclables is not only available in the booking process.

Also, users are provided with a variety of options on how to accomplish their tasks.Open schedules and My Upcoming Schedules are moved to the homepage to be easily seen instead of clicking the Book Now button first.

My Upcoming Schedules will also display a status below the date to easily communicate the pick-up or booking status and reduce user effort.

#2 - Cancel booking

Map - to provide them with the real-time location of the collectors. This way customers can easily visualize where the collectors are in case they got lost.

Cancel button - I added this on the Booking summary for ease of access, and added a note when the button will be disabled. This can also be easily seen by Trash Panda.

#3 - System status & Feedback

Date and status - I added the status below the date to maintain design consistency and provide immediate status.

I also added confirmation popups to let the users know that the system received their requests or an option to undo request.

#4 - Progress trackers

I replaced the step numbers on the top left part of the booking process with a progress tracker or wizard to help the users know how many steps they have to complete, where they are in the process, and which steps they have completed.

Usability Testing

To test the changes made to the current design, I asked the usability participants to do these tasks again and aim to:

  • See if the solution targets the initial problems.
  • Uncover usability problems with the new design.
  • Observe their reaction and how users adjust to the change.

What Users have to say

“This design is a huge improvement to the current app as it addresses the areas that the app could improve on.”

“The design makes task simpler since its all available now.”

“It was a bit new to me but its good that there are other option to check this instead of waiting for an open schedule.”

Key insights

  • Users found it easier to access information
  • There's evidence of users adjusting to the changes on the first task.
  • Users like that everything is available now in the app.
  • Some users are exploring other paths in accomplishing tasks.

Cashback

I went ahead to design the 2 features that are currently not implemented in the app.

Cashback - where they can process the withdrawal of their balance to their preferred e-wallets or bank. They can also see their transaction history and a quick summary of their request at the end of the process before confirming.

The form is modeled close to Gcash(a common e-wallet app in the Philippines) for familiarity.

Coaching corner

I moved the coaching corner to the hamburger menu to have a dedicated area for it and give way to the schedules, and support link.

Booking History

Previously, customers will need to check their email to check the booking details. Currently, there is no means for the customer to check which recycling facility did their recyclables ended up in.

What Users have to say

“It’s good so I don’t have to check my email for the booking”

“This is very useful”

“I like this. Everything is available in the app.”

Reflections and lessons learned

#1 The taste of the real experience

I started very nervous but very excited about this project because I treated this as a real one. After the process, I feel very fulfilled and excited to think about the next case study. This helped me to realize that I:

  • Enjoy generating ideas or solutions.
  • Sketching those solutions.
  • Keeping the users and the business aligned.
  • It's okay to fail as long as you learn from it.

From that, I was able to drop the things I overthink about and did the best I could.

#2 Recruitment and user research

It was a good experience to talk and reach out to total strangers for the interview, it gave me an idea of how a real user interview will be and prepared me for the next case study.

The recruitment was more of a challenge than the interview since I relied on Facebook page rather than joining groups that have the same goal as Trash Panda, at the same time.

#3 Usability testing

It's amazing how users adjust to, which for me, is a small change. This proves that not every redesign could be successful.

I consider this a success because I was not aiming to improve the time on task but convenience. Efficiency just came as a result of providing what the users need in the tasks.
